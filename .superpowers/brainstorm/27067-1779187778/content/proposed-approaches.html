Proposed Approaches

Based on your focus on text-heavy documents and the need for "perfect" numeric verification.

Approach 1: The "Agentic Auditor"

Uses a two-step verification chain to ensure amounts are correct.

  • Ingestion: PDF → Markdown (via LlamaParse/Marker) → Vector DB.
  • Retrieval: Hybrid search (BM25 + Vector).
  • Generation: Agent A extracts the answer; Agent B "audits" the answer by looking at the raw source chunks again.

Pros

  • Highest accuracy for amounts
  • Self-correcting

Cons

  • Higher latency
  • Higher token cost

Approach 2: "Citation-First RAG"

Forces the system to prove every amount with a direct quote.

  • Ingestion: PDF → Structured Chunks (preserving page numbers/headers).
  • Retrieval: Context-aware retrieval (parent/child chunks).
  • Generation: LLM must provide [Quote] and [Page Number] for every amount.
  • Validation: Programmatic check to ensure the quote exists in the source.

Pros

  • Faster than Agentic
  • Easy for humans to verify

Cons

  • Relies on LLM following strict citation rules
